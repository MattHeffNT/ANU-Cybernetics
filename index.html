Where my mob are from

Papunya/Warumpi

My family come from around this small remote community in central Australia called Papunya or Warumpi and further west out to communities like Mount Leibig, Kintore and Kiwirrkura. I spent the first few years of my life here. It is the birthplace of the "dot painting" art style and The Warumpi Band (famous for the song My Island Home). The area and mob that I come from are the last to have had "first contact" with white Australians, with the last group of Pintupi in 1984. A bit different to the narrative of colonization happening hundreds of years ago right?

Indigital - what we do, NBE, what it means

So I want to talk a little bit about Indigital, and some of the ways in which we're using tech to inspire and disrupt the education system. I think one of the downsides of western or white education is that everything is compartmentalized into their respective electives or disciplines etc. Even though this isn't necessarily 1. the way the real world works, and 2. The way our brain approaches problem solving. Related to this, is the way in which "Indigenous things" are taught as ideas, events and moments in history. Even though Indigenous people are still here, expressing our culture in new and unique ways. Indigital has a few different "arms", but our main product is our schools program. Firstly, we reach out to the respective traditional owners and elders of the area in which the school is.

Minecraft Education

We work with teachers and show them how to leverage Minecraft Education Edition which has a Python,Javascript and "MakeCode" interface. We get students to use coding to print out words in the respective Indigenous languages. Or explore the traditional Indigenous stories from the area and how they could be imagined through these worlds. Depending on the class, this can scale in complexity.

Voice Recorder

We couple this process with Paint3D or TinkerCAD, where we introduce teachers or students to 3d art and modelling. We then use our voice recorder app to record the stories or just words in Indigenous languages. The MineCraft world, the 3d Models and voice reccordings are then all brought together in our mobile application which proivdes an Augmented reality view.

    Indigi-Futurism

    Traditional eel Trap - Victoria

    Indigi-Futurism is a subgenre of sci-fi, and like other "futurist" fiction can include speculative history and speculative fiction.....

    What seperates it from its literary cousins however is the idea that we're living in our post-apocalyptic dystopias future now.....it's hard to ignore that after seeing images of the red skies from the bush fires not long ago, or when thinking about the impact of nuclear testing on Indigenous families in South Australia.

    What fascinates me is...Tjukurrpa or "dreamtime" stories aren't just fairy tails or fables....they also served a purpose for our survival....with new modes of expressing identity, how do we create new tjukurrpa? Do your cultures or sub-cultures (broadlY not just ethnic cultures) have storytelling as a means by which to express identity, or to share survival information?

    I like to imagine what this country could look like if we started paying attention to the "why" of traditional Indigenous engineering systems

    I also like to question why mental models of Indigenous cultural expression have limited us to dot paintings, didgereedoos and clap sticks. While these are important, i think these new modalities of identity, and tjukurrpa are just as "authentic" as the aforementioned objects that are often associated with our culture. Imagine if your cultural expression was reduced to the object, custom that your culture/s is most famous for?
