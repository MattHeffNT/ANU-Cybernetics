Matt

So my name is Matt I'm a Luritja/Arrernte and German/Irish mix. in my language and culture I have a few names. My formal name is Ngamurarri after my famous painter uncle "Mick Ngamurarri" of the Papunya Tula arts movement. I have a few other names like Tjapaltjarri, and Kalibabu . But you can just call me Matt.

Where my mob are from

My family come from around this small remote community in central Australia called Papunya or Warumpi and further west. I spent the first few years of my life here. It is the birthplace of the "dot painting" art style and The Warumpi Band (famous for the song My Island Home).

My interest in computers

Me...mad chillin' circa 2004

I was first exposed to computers through my dad, who had an old laptop that was the size of a small brief case. I was exposed to DOS, Basic and Space Invaders. Growing up, in Alice Springs and later in Darwin, my interest in video games grew, so I went about learning Basic and C to try and program a text adventure. I did some IT in school, but dropped it as an elective because i found it boring. However, wasn't until i worked in the NT Government in the Dept of Business in the economcis and policy team that I found myself leaning on programming again.....for some reason, the department at the time, was manually downloading ABS data sheets, and manually doing the number crunches which would then be loaded into respective graphs and reports for things like the state of the states and quarterly business updates blah blah. This process would take approximately a week to pull all together. So I went about automating this by scraping the ABS website for the respective data (in retrospect I should have just used the API), I automated the calculations and then automated the populating of the graphs. A four day job turned into a 3 minute job with a press of a button. This made me realize, I actually enjoyed the coding much more than being a public servant. from there I went into an IT degree with a software developer specialization, and have had the opportunity to work across a variety of interesting projects and agencies.
